# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct (Strict Anti-Private-Interest & Traceability Edition)

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to ensure that all participation  
in this community is free from harassment, discrimination, **and any form of private interest interference**.

We explicitly commit that:
- No individual or group may use this community for undisclosed personal, financial, political, or organizational gain.
- All decisions, contributions, and governance actions must be **traceable, reviewable, and attributable**.
- Community integrity, transparency, and accountability take precedence over all individual interests.

## Core Principles

- **Zero tolerance for undisclosed conflicts of interest**
- **Mandatory traceability of all meaningful actions and data flows**
- **Accountability over anonymity in governance and decision-making**
- **Public-interest-first decision standard**

## Our Standards

Positive behaviors include:

- Acting in the best interest of the community at all times  
- Proactively disclosing any potential conflicts of interest  
- Ensuring that all significant actions (code, moderation, governance decisions) are **logged and attributable**  
- Maintaining verifiable records of decisions and their rationale  
- Challenging decisions that lack transparency or appear self-serving  

Unacceptable behaviors include:

- Any attempt to derive **undisclosed personal, financial, or reputational benefit**  
- Concealing, misrepresenting, or failing to disclose conflicts of interest  
- Using privileged access (data, moderation tools, governance power) for private gain  
- Making decisions without proper traceability or auditability  
- Tampering with, deleting, or obscuring logs or historical records  
- Engaging in favoritism, collusion, or coordinated influence  
- Introducing unverifiable or non-traceable data into the system  

## Conflict of Interest Policy (Strict)

All participants must:

- Fully disclose any **direct or indirect interests** related to their contributions  
- Update disclosures when circumstances change  
- Publicly declare conflicts in relevant discussions or decisions  

Strict prohibitions:

- Participation in decisions where any conflict exists (mandatory recusal)  
- Use of intermediaries to bypass conflict-of-interest restrictions  
- Retroactive disclosure after influence has already been exerted  

Violations will be treated as severe breaches of community trust.

## Data Traceability & Audit Requirements

To ensure accountability, the project enforces:

- **End-to-end traceability** of all meaningful data generated, modified, or used within the project  
- All contributions must be linked to:
  - A verifiable identity (individual or accountable entity)  
  - A timestamp  
  - A change history  
- **Immutable or append-only logging mechanisms** must be used where feasible  
- Any data used in decision-making must be:
  - Source-attributable  
  - Reproducible where applicable  
- Deletion or modification of records must itself be logged and justified  

Non-traceable contributions may be rejected or removed.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders must:

- Enforce rules **without personal bias or benefit**  
- Publicly document significant enforcement decisions and their rationale  
- Recuse themselves automatically in cases of potential conflict  
- Submit their own actions to the same traceability and audit standards  

Failure of leaders to meet these requirements constitutes a violation.

## Scope

This Code applies to:

- All community spaces  
- All governance and moderation processes  
- All technical systems and data flows  
- Any context in which the community or its outputs are represented  

## Enforcement

Reports may include:

- Harassment or misconduct  
- Conflicts of interest  
- Lack of traceability or data integrity violations  
- Abuse of authority or opaque decision-making  

All reports must be:

- Logged  
- Investigated with traceable processes  
- Resolved with documented outcomes  

Anonymous reports may be accepted, but **actions taken must still be auditable**.

## Enforcement Guidelines

### 1. Correction
Minor issues or first-time traceability/conflict lapses.  
→ Required disclosure, correction, and documentation.

### 2. Warning
Clear violation or failure to disclose interests.  
→ Formal warning, restricted participation, mandatory recusal.

### 3. Temporary Ban
Serious breach, concealment, or traceability violation.  
→ Temporary removal and audit of past actions.

### 4. Permanent Ban
Systematic abuse, deliberate opacity, or exploitation of the system.  
→ Permanent removal and potential public disclosure of misconduct.
